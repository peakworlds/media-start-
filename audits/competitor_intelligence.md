# Competitor Intelligence & Battlecards

**Company:** Peak Haven Australia
**Date:** 2026-03-06

---

## 1. Competitive Landscape Overview

The Australian market for research peptides is fragmented into three highly distinct tiers:
1. **The Telehealth/Compounding Monopolizers:** High-end clinics leveraging AHPRA-registered doctors to prescribe Schedule 4 medications. Massive markups. High trust. Complete legal compliance.
2. **The Dropship Grey Market (The Hustlers):** Shopify stores importing bulk powders from Alibaba/China and slapping neon gym labels on them. Low trust. High risk of ABF seizure (Customs). Wildly fluctuating purity (60%-80%). Marketing on Instagram.
3. **The "Shadow Lab" (Peak Haven's Target Wedge):** The exact middle ground. Grey market legal footing ("Research Only"), but operating with extreme verification (Janoshik) and precision cold-chain infrastructure that rivals Tier-1 pharma.

## 2. Competing with "The Doctors" (Telehealth Clinics)

### Hidden Weakness
They are outrageously expensive gatekeepers. A 10mg vial of BPC-157 synthesized domestically costs $15. The clinic charges a $250 "consultation fee" and then marks the vial up to $600. Furthermore, users hate having their biohacking stack logged into the Australian Digital Health Record (My Health Record). 

### The Reframing Questions (For Inner Haven Support DMs)
*   **The Mark-Up Probe:** "Have you actually looked at the raw mass spectrometry data for what [Clinic Name] is dispensing, or are you just paying an $800 premium because a doctor handed it to you?"
*   **The Surveillance Probe:** "Do you prefer having your experimental metabolic stacks logged permanently against your government health profile, or holding control of your own research protocols?"

### Positioning Landmine to Avoid
**Do NOT claim medical equivalence.** Never say "Our peptides are exactly what doctors prescribe." This triggers immediate TGA raids. Instead, say: "We provide >99% analytical-grade structural scaffolds to independent researchers."

## 3. Competing with "The Dropshippers" (Grey Market)

### Hidden Weakness
They are lying about their purity and their logistics. They claim "Australian Stock" but drop-ship from Shenzhen. Their products melt in transit across the equator and sit in hot mailboxes. They fake their Trustpilot reviews and they *never* publish live, independent HPLC test links because their purity fluctuates wildly per batch.

### The Reframing Questions (For Communities/Reddit)
*   **The Heat Probe:** "Do you actually inject compounds that spent 14 days in a hot cargo hold from China? Where is their thermal tracking data?"
*   **The Verification Probe:** "Have you ever tried to scan the QR code on [Competitor Name]'s 'Certificate of Analysis'? It leads to a dead link, not Janoshik's database."

### Positioning Landmine to Avoid
**Do NOT compete on price.** If [Competitor X] sells TB-500 for $45, do not drop to $40. Peak Haven sells it for $120. If you engage in a price war, you legitimize the competitor. Instead, reframe the $45 vial as toxic garbage. "A $45 vial means they completely skipped the $500 batch purity test. Good luck to your liver."
