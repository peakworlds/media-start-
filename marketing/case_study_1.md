# SCQA Case Study: The 12-Hour Cold-Chain Integrity Test

**Company:** Peak Haven Australia
**Date:** 2026-03-06
**Format:** Use for the "Cold-Chain Logistics" webpage and Telegram drops.

---

## 1. Situation (The Baseline Reality)
The Australian peptide grey-market is defined by cheap prices and catastrophic thermal logistics. A standard vial of Retatrutide purchased from a domestic dropshipper travels from Shenzhen (ambient shipping, ~14 days), sits in a 40°C Australian customs warehouse, and is eventually shipped via standard parcel post across the country. 

By the time the end-user reconstitutes the vial, mass spectrometry analysis reveals the peptide structure has degraded by over 30%, rendering it biologically ineffective or potentially hepatotoxic due to broken amino acid chains.

## 2. Complication (The "Peak Haven" Standard)
Peak Haven operates on a single promise: Uncompromising, verified purity. To charge a 40% premium for analytical-grade materials, we had to prove that our cold-chain infrastructure actually worked during the peak of an Australian summer.

The hypothesis: Could we dispatch a highly fragile, temperature-sensitive array (Phase II Metabolic Agonist protocol) from our East Coast Node to a rural Western Australia address in the middle of January, while maintaining an internal temperature below 5°C?

## 3. Question
If we embed a medical-grade Bluetooth temperature data logger inside the thermal mailer alongside the lyophilized vials and send it across the continent via Express Transit, will the cold-chain break? 

*And if it does break, what does that mean for the 90% of suppliers who don't even use ice?*

## 4. Answer (The Execution & Results)

### The Setup
1. Two vials of Tirzepatide (Batch #PH-T04, pre-verified at 99.4% purity by Janoshik) were extracted from the Node Prime -20°C freezer at 11:30 AM.
2. The vials, alongside a Govee Thermal Logger, were sealed in Mylar, sandwiched between two -15°C pharmaceutical gel packs, and vacuum-sealed in a 5mm polyurethane bubble mailer.
3. The package was dispatched via Toll Priority Air Express at 2:00 PM. 

### The Transit Log
*   **0 Hours (Brisbane):** Internal temp logs at -2°C upon dispatch. Ambient exterior temperature: 34°C.
*   **8 Hours (In Flight):** Internal temp stabilizes at 2°C in the unpressurized cargo hold.
*   **18 Hours (Perth Sorting Facility):** Package arrives at the depot. Ambient exterior temperature: 28°C. Internal temp logs at 4°C. The vacuum seal prevents external thermal bleed.
*   **26 Hours (Final Delivery - Kalgoorlie WA):** The package is handed to the client.

### The Results
When the client cracked the vacuum seal, the internal thermal logger read **4.8°C**. The gel packs were still semi-solid. The vials had never exceeded refrigerated temperatures during a 3,500km journey across the hottest continent on Earth.
